\# BlackKnight One Operations Engine



\## Purpose



The Operations Engine supports the daily identity work that enterprise teams perform after the environment has been built.



Most IAM projects focus on configuration. BlackKnight One also focuses on operational reality.



People join.

People move.

People leave.

Contractors expire.

Executives need urgent access.

Accounts become compromised.

Engineers need evidence.



The Operations Engine exists to turn those events into repeatable, validated, and reportable workflows.



\## Operating Model



Every workflow follows three phases:



\### Provision



Create or change the identity state.



\### Validate



Confirm the expected changes completed successfully.



\### Report



Generate evidence that the workflow completed correctly.



This supports the BlackKnight One principle:



> Building confidence through continuous verification.



\## Operations Pillars



\### Workforce Lifecycle Engine



Handles user lifecycle events such as onboarding, transfers, promotions, terminations, and contractor access.



\### Partner Operations Engine



Supports delegated administration and partner ecosystem workflows such as GDAP validation, cross-tenant access review, and customer inventory.



\### License Operations Engine



Supports license readiness, license optimization, orphaned license detection, and group-based licensing validation.



\### Identity Requests Engine



Supports day-to-day identity requests such as group access, temporary access, guest access, shared mailbox access, and distribution list access.



\### Incident Response Engine



Supports urgent identity actions such as emergency termination, compromised account response, privileged access removal, and evidence reporting.



\## North Star



The Operations Engine answers one question:



> Can I prove this identity operation completed correctly?
